NBCOlympics.com -- 2004 Summer Games

  • GOAL: Build the premier Olympics web site and create a convergence vehicle for NBC's multi-platform delivery of the 2004 Olympic games.
  • PROCESS: Work with NBC to define goals. Merge technologies from a number of companies. Crank up staffing and move the office to Athens for a month. Work like crazy. Then sleep in September.
  • NOTABLE: Real-Time Results, Countries, DRM VideoPlayer, Slideshow, Interactive Flash Video, Educational Games, Wireless Content, etc.
  • OUTCOME: One of the largest web sites on the internet in the summer of 2004 with 20 million unique users. Ground-breaking convergence and revenue that created buzz from The New Yorker to business, design, and technology trade publications.

Content Management System

  • GOAL: Create an easy-to-use yet robust interface for quick content management that is completely separate from display mechanisms.
  • MEDIUM: At one point or another, this uses everything. Primarily, it uses DHTML for the GUI, ColdFusion for the applications, and Java for content-generation.
  • PROCESS: Our CMS is a constantly-evolving beast. It is extremely dynamic, and we recently added a Flash-based "CMS Lite" for our tv journalists who are intimidated by the functionality of the primary interface.
  • OUTCOME: Ultimately, our robust CMS has been one of the big selling points for many of our partners, including NBC, Cox, and Washington Post. It allows for easy content-sharing while still maintaining strict access control.

Multimedia Components

  • GOAL: Create a series of templated multimedia components that anyone can use to quickly create interactive content. A second goal was to explore ways to better integrate interactive content into stories, rather than creating more popup links.
  • MEDIUM: Flash, XML, ColdFusion.
  • PROCESS: A journalist uses a WYSIWYG editor that outputs XML. The Flash file is inserted into story pages and makes the request for the XML. All of this is done with very little editorial work.
  • OUTCOME: The component tableau now includes: Timeline, Image Map, Pie Chart, Line Chart, Bar Graph, Calendar, Audio Slideshow, MP3 Player, and Headline Scroller.

The Euro

  • GOAL: Create an interactive experience to help an American to understand and engage with the transition of much of Western Europe to the Euro.
  • MEDIUM: Flash.
  • PROCESS: The primary goal was to approximate the real, tactile experience of "touching" the foreign Euro bills in a virtual environment.
  • OUTCOME: The dragable magnifier was successful -- I was surprised how quickly people "got" this potentially intimidating interface. A possible improvement might be the addition of a personal relationship to currency itself. Perhaps a survey that asks how attached people are to the dollar bill, or a political questions about sacrificing national identity for economic unity.

Hurricane Tracker

  • GOAL: Create a hurricane tracker that parses NWS data dynamically and presents it with a strong visual model.
  • MEDIUM: Java and XML.
  • PROCESS: This is one of my forays into using client-side Java, and I would probably do it in Flash now. But Java has certainly been successful at number-crunching and visualizing at those moments when I have the least time to fix a broken product.
  • OUTCOME: Since setting it up, the app has remained completely automated. That's a huge success alone. Meteorologists love it too -- also a rarity.

Menu Builder

  • GOAL: Turn a large USGA food database into a searchable menu-building application.
  • MEDIUM: Custom database-driven application. It includes customizable features in the form of "Your Menu."
  • PROCESS: I started to write this in ASP, but it ended up being easier for us to support with ColdFusion.
  • OUTCOME: It's good for what it is, but there are features I'd like to add, such as the ability to store your menu items for future use and build a calendar/diary of food consumption.

Election 2002: Balance Of Power

  • GOAL: Create a live election night interface for national election results and a balance-of-power indicator.
  • MEDIUM: Perl script translates data feed from AP and writes out XML files. Flash makes direct requests to the XML, which it parses race-by-race.
  • PROCESS: I manage our entire election coverage, which was extremely focused on local content. However, this dynamic application was a way to offer a quick cross-section of what's happening nationally on election night.
  • OUTCOME: It was extremely fast, because there was no caching attached to the XML files. Drilling down into information on the states could have been made easier.
