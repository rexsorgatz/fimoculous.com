December 11-16, 2020
Matt Roush
TV Insider

What to watch in a pandemic? Ricky Gervais is back with the second season of After Life on Netflix. American Idol has its finale. And The Great takes liberties with the story of Catherine the Great.

You can also tune into the TV Insider Podcast, where this week I discuss these shows in-depth.

Have a great week!

After Life

FRIDAY, APRIL 24 • NETFLIX

Ricky Gervais returns in a second season of his emotionally overwhelming dramedy of loss and the healing power of human kindness. As misanthropic widower Tony, he's still wallowing in grief while watching videos of his late wife, but with quirky and caring friends inspiring him to pay their generosity forward, and maybe even start a relationship with his dying dad's nurse (Ashley Jensen), life's too short not to root for this bloke.

Defending Jacob

FRIDAY, APRIL 24 • APPLE TV+

A strong cast elevates this grim legal drama, based on William Landay's novel, about a teenager (it's Jaeden Martell) who's charged with a classmate's murder and how the accusation shatters his parents' world. Chris Evans is his father, an assistant district attorney who'll go to desperate measures to clear Jacob's name, and Downton Abbey's Michelle Dockery is riveting as his mother, unsettled by not knowing what her own child could be capable of.

Snowpiercer

Sunday, May 17, 9/8c • TNT

Based on the graphic novels and subsequent film by Parasite's Bong Joon-ho, this propulsive sci-fi action series is set aboard a 10-mile-long speeding train that carries what's left of humanity on a non-stop thrill ride through a frozen-wasteland Earth. On Snowpiercer, the inequality between first class and steerage is stoking social revolution, which could complicate a murder investigation for the last surviving homicide cop (Hamilton's Daveed Diggs).

The Great

Friday, May 15 • Hulu

Turning Catherine the Great's rise to power into a broadly satirical "occasionally true story," writer/producer Tony McNamara (The Favourite) takes considerable creative license in his account of the young future monarch (Elle Fanning). She's in for a shock when she arrives in Russia and takes the measure of her immature fool of a husband, Emperor Peter (Nicholas Hoult).
