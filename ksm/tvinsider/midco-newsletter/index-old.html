To help you choose which shows are best to entertain your kids of various ages and grade levels while also teaching them a few things, please enjoy this monthly guide, brought to you from Midco!

For Younger Learners

Elinor Wonders Why

Weekdays on PBS Kids (check local listings)

This animated series for preschoolers aims to encourage children to follow their curiosity, ask questions when they don’t understand and fi nd answers using science inquiry skills.

Explore a New Art Class

Available on HBO Max

HBO Max, a new streaming service that launched earlier this year, has oodles of interactive, educational programming to hold your kids’ attention and challenge their creativity.

Ready for Preschool

Disney Junior’s YouTube Channel

Disney Junior’s Ready for Preschool short-form series has amassed nearly 21.5 million video views on Disney Junior’s YouTube channel.

For Older Learners
(And Curious Adults)

First Ladies

CNN, Sundays at 10pm

CNN’s original Series about America’s most iconic first ladies, is narrated by Golden Globewinning actress Robin Wright, and profi les Michelle Obama, Jackie Kennedy, Nancy Reagan, Eleanor Roosevelt, Lady Bird Johnson, and Hillary Rodham Clinton.

Nova: "Can We Fool The Planet?"

PBS, Oct. 28 at 9pm

As global temperatures rise, scientists are exploring geoengineering solutions, from planting trees to sucking carbon out of the air to physically blocking out sunlight. But would it work? And what are the risks of engineering Earth’s climate?
Did You Know?

Many students are most fresh in the morning, making it a good time to learn! Kahn Academy is a free online resource you can find at kahnacademy.org. You’ll find countless tutorials (instructed by teachers and experts) to support just about any subject and grade level. It’s an amazing site offering practice exercises, instructional videos and a personalized learning dashboard that empower learners to study at their own pace in and outside of the classroom.
