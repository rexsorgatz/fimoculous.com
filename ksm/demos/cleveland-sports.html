The Browns find a new way to lose, and that's not easy -- Bud Shaw

Updated December 11, 2017 at 8:48 AM; Posted December 11, 2017 at 8:43 AM
Cleveland Browns quarterback DeShone Kizer against Green Bay Packers, December 10, 2017
8

Gallery: Cleveland Browns quarterback DeShone Kizer against Green Bay Packers, December 10, 2017

By Bud Shaw, cleveland.com

CLEVELAND, Ohio - On second thought...

As the losses have piled high over two seasons, you might think the Browns completely exhausted new ways to lose.

And you'd be as wrong as the prognosticator (me) who keeps picking the Browns to win.

The Browns did blow two 14-point leads last season but never as late as the fourth quarter. One of those games (Baltimore) was at home. The other was against Pittsburgh in the season finale when Landry Jones quarterbacked the Steelers.

Sunday's 27-21 overtime loss to Green Bay didn't happen against Aaron Rodgers. It happened against Brett Hundley who has fewer career starts than DeShone Kizer.

"It's a broken record," Hue Jackson said of his team's 13th consecutive loss and 28th in the last 29 games. "They fight hard."

The record that looms for the Browns - a 0-16 season done only once by the 2008 Detroit Lions - is a record not meant to be broken.

Only shamefully tied.

* * *

The Browns are solidifying the top spot in the draft, now owning a 99 percent chance of securing it according to ESPN's Football Power Index.

The Browns nearest competitors won Sunday. San Francisco, for the second consecutive Jimmy Garoppolo start, beat Houston. Chicago, behind Mitch Trubisky, rolled Cincinnati on the road.

Houston's free fall since losing quarterback Deshaun Watson for the season means the Browns, who own the Texans' first pick, will have two of the top six or seven choices in the 2018 draft.

I know what you're thinking but, no, they shouldn't take quarterbacks with both selections.

* * *

The Bengals didn't seem to have much fight after their brawling loss to the Steelers a week earlier.

Cincinnati's players expressed surprise, though, at the size of the loss to Chicago.

From Pro Football Talk: "(but) it's hard to be surprised when a 5-7 team loses in any fashion."

So how can we claim surprise that a 0-12 team loses a 14-point lead in the fourth quarter?

There. I knew that would make you feel better.

* * *

A late stat from the Steelers-Ravens game Sunday night after the Steelers appeared on the verge of losing a 14-point lead. (They eventually won 39-38.)

Announced on Westwood One: the Steelers record at home in games they led by two touchdowns.

It was (and still is) 215-0-2.

Hue Jackson and the Browns since 2016: 0-2.

* * *

Josh Gordon caught three passes for 69 yards and a TD.

Corey Coleman caught five passes for 62 yards and a TD.

Browns' touchdown sunglasses explained

Each donned sunglasses on the sidelines after their scores.

"I think the celebrations will go times 10 when we do win," said rookie quarterback DeShone Kizer.

So you have that to bring you back.

* * *

If you want and expect the Browns to play with enthusiasm and passion in an awful season, you shouldn't be critical of TD celebrations just because they're winless.

Unless they choreograph a full Broadway musical and get a delay of game penalty.

* * *

Jackson: "There is more there in DeShone."

We think he meant more three-touchdown games and not more interceptions. He leads the league with 17 picks with three games to go.

* * *

Gordon's TD catch was his first since Dec. 15, 2013.

For perspective, that was so long ago Jameis Winston won the Heisman.

And has 64 TDs since.

* * *

Isaiah Crowell rushed for 119 yards. If that seems like the first 100 yard game of the season for Crowell, it is.

The Browns ran 27 times (two by Kizer). Kizer threw 28 passes, just 10 after halftime.

Turns out they would've been better off throwing nine.

Most impressive offensive stat for the Browns: three scoring drives of 88, 70 and 84 yards.

Least impressive stat: 0-13.

* * *

Kenny Britt's agent, Fadde Mikhail, took to Twitter with some choice comments about Jackson after the loss to Green Bay:

Another L for a part time HC, Full time Beat Writer !

-- FADDE (@fadde) December 10, 2017

Coaches that talk to media more than their players <

-- FADDE (@fadde) December 10, 2017

Jackson is a big talker for sure. His media friendliness has no doubt spared him legitimate criticsm at times.

But then again, we're talking Kenny Britt, who collected $10.5 million for 18 catches this season.

There's something worse than being ripped by Britt. It's being aligned with him.
