ISSUE 27 • August 17, 2020
The new rules around personal space
Social distancing has changed the way we pass one another in supermarket aisles, on the sidewalk, and in the workplace. Now we all have to rethink the nonverbal cues and rules we’ve followed our entire lives. One term worth learning: proxemics, the study of interpersonal space. As we return to the workplace, it’s important for leaders and team members to have open and honest conversations about proxemics. That can help alleviate the stress of potentially intruding on someone else’s safe space—and make sure your own safe space is respected.

City of murals
Buenos Aires is a city of colorful murals—at last count, there were more than 400 painted on the facades of buildings, public squares, and interior walls of Argentina’s bustling capital. So it made sense that murals and art would play a key role in the identity of WeWork Corrientes 800. The WeWork design team tapped Argentinean artist Hernan Paganini, who created a graphic red and white mural for the multistory space. It reflects “the relationship between the places, history, and politics,” says the artist—and creates a vibrant, meaningful focal point for all who visit the space.
