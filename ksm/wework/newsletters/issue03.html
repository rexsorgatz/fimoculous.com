What Glamsquad’s CEO does all day
Amy Shecter, the CEO of the on-demand, app-based beauty service Glamsquad, breaks down her sometimes-hectic-but-always-exciting daily routine. The early riser (she gets to the office at WeWork 31 St. James Ave in Boston by 7:30 a.m.) starts her day by catching up with the company’s client experience team, an important meeting because of how closely she empathizes with her customers. In the rare case Glamsquad has to make a late cancellation, Shecter feels more stressed than usual. “I take it very personally,” she says. “I think of myself with wet hair, in my apartment, waiting for a blowout. I don’t want to leave any woman in that situation.”

Work Style
As physical spaces evolve, so does the way we dress. Carley Fortune, executive editor of Refinery29 Canada, loves how her office, located at WeWork 100 University Ave in Toronto, champions self-expression. “People wear whatever they’re comfortable in,” Fortune says. “If you want to be super fashion-y one day, you’ll do it. If it’s jeans, a T-shirt, and no makeup, that’s fine. Sometimes, during lunch, I try to sneak out for a 20-minute run, and I will sit in my running clothes all afternoon. I don’t take for granted the freedom we have.”
