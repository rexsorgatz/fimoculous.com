ISSUE 26 • August 3, 2020
The future of the commute
One of the biggest challenges in bringing employees back to the office right now is figuring out how to get them there in the safest way possible. The long commute on crowded public transportation needs a swift rethink. Three top mobility experts—representing Scoop Technologies, a carpooling platform; Commutifi, which reports on commuter behavior for businesses; and Swiftly, which uses real-time commuting data—share the innovative solutions we can expect to see in the near future.

In recent months, brokers want to know about the future of flexible workspace, what WeWork is doing differently to keep their clients safe, and how, exactly, we’re future-proofing our real estate deals in these still-uncertain times. How media and entertainment companies are getting back to work
Global demand for content is surging, and media and entertainment companies are exploring new ways to create content while social distancing. WeWork has helped develop custom production spaces that allow creators to spread out, collaborate, and get back to doing what they love.
What to do when you outgrow HQ
When ride-hailing app Citymobil outgrew its Leningradsky, Russia, location, it set up developers in a nearby WeWork location. Customized with large conference rooms, a 24-hour pantry for employees that worked different shifts, and quiet spaces for engineers, the space soon attracted other departments as well.
New ways to think about the workplace during COVID-19
The global pandemic has made us all rethink the role of the physical office. Businesses are now spreading out within one location, decentralizing offices, and offering employees options to work near home to reduce commutes. Read more about how they’re doing just that.
