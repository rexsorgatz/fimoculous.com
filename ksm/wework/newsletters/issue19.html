ISSUE 19 • April 27, 2020
Makers step in to fill the PPE shortage when factories can’t
The COVID-19 pandemic has left healthcare, frontline, and other essential workers without the personal protective equipment (PPE) they need to do their jobs safely. While large manufacturers have pledged to step up production, their very size means it will take time for them to refocus their machinery and factories. In the meantime, groups like Tel Aviv, Israel–based maker community Tikkun Olam Makers (TOM) are jumping in to produce much-needed materials fast and efficiently, using 3D printing technology.

Around the world, the food and beverage industry has been upended in many ways because of the coronavirus pandemic. But for some companies, that disruption has inspired innovative pivots to help others in need. New initiatives from these businesses provide meals to seniors in isolation, redirect fresh food from now-empty offices to food banks, facilitate virtual dinners where people can connect, and more. In doing so, these companies are taking care of their employees, their businesses, and their communities.
