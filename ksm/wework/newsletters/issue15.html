Entrepreneurs on a mission to improve public systems
These founders are using technology to improve the way two very different public systems work: Marcus Bullock founded Flikshop, an app offering a groundbreaking method of connecting inmates with their family and friends; Yemi Adewunmi and her cofounder Damola Ogundipe conceived Civic Eagle, a platform making it easier for policy professionals to access and interpret legislation. They share their stories—and what inspired them to fix policies and institutions they clearly saw as broken.
MEMBER SPOTLIGHT
Bacardi
Mexico City, Mexico
When legendary spirits brand Bacardi wanted to get closer to its customer base in Mexico City, it decided to move right into a vibrant neighborhood where energetic millennials live, work, and play. “Scouting a location, you think, I need to be close to the consumer,” says Constantino Spas, Bacardi’s vice president and managing director of Latin America and the Caribbean. “We looked at where most of the young people in Mexico City are spending their time.” Bacardi's timing was impeccable: WeWork was expanding into Latin America by opening office space in Mexico City. Bacardi moved its team of 40 into a private office at WeWork Varsovia. Now not only is the team located in the trendy heart of the city but they’re also working in the same building and interacting with creative, smart entrepreneurs who resemble their core consumers.
