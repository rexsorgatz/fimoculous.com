ISSUE 06 • OCT 28, 2019
When a vendor let me down, I decided to launch my own business
Zaki Hussain never thought he’d be a business owner. “I used to think business founders were a special breed of person,” he writes. “I felt they knew something I didn’t.” But when an outside consultancy completely flubbed a project and it was up to Hussain to fix it, he experienced one of those aha moments. It didn’t stop the fear and self-doubt from creeping in, but he pushed through. Today, he is the CEO of his very own marketing consulting agency, Clutchgrowth, a member at WeWork Riverpark Tower in San Jose, CA.

A design college finds space for the creators of tomorrow
Raffles Design International is one of Asia’s top design colleges. Since 1990, it’s grown from one college in Singapore to 22 institutions in 12 countries. For its new space in Mumbai, it was important for the design to reflect the great potential of the school’s creative students. That’s where WeWork came in—and had a little bit of fun.

Lunch Break
The communications and culture manager whose lunch ritual is born from her subscription farm box
Welcome to “Lunch Break,” a new series that examines how members break bread around the world. Lisa Marie Schnaubelt, who works at beer company Fort Point and goes by LM, skips the downtown San Francisco lunch hustle near their WeWork building in favor of a home-cooked meal made with vegetables from her CSA (community supported agriculture) subscription farm box. In her previous job as a teacher, everyone packed their lunch for work, and the habit stuck. Still, LM always finds a way to make last night’s leftovers even more flavorful for her midday meal.
