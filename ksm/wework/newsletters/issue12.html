Harnessing the power of community
Isis Miller, a community team member at WeWork Salesforce Tower in San Francisco, learned at a volunteering event at a local high school that two students, siblings Robert and Saundra Buyard, were on the verge of becoming homeless. Driven to do what she could to help, Miller launched a GoFundMe to try to cover the siblings’ basic needs. “I would like us to ban together as a community and support these teens and their family,” Miller wrote. “They deserve a chance to be able to fulfill their potential, but that will never happen if they aren't given the support they need.” News of the fund reached the local news, as well as WeWork’s executive chair Marcelo Claure, who announced that he would match donations. The fund raised over $39,000. That meant the siblings could afford to rent a new apartment and sleep under one roof.
MEMBER SPOTLIGHT
IBM CIO Office
London, UK
IBM’s CIO Office was looking to bring employees back into the office after years of working remotely. They wanted a space where their diverse body of employees could feel comfortable and where collaboration could happen easily. After considering their options, IBM chose to unite their team by taking over the entire seventh floor of WeWork 10 York Road in Waterloo, London. With a variety of seating, a large communal area, desks near windows, and a moss wall that brings the outside in, the office is optimized for employee productivity. “We were looking for something that supported our vision of being a really attractive place to work, but also design it in such a way that would suit the type of work we’re trying to do across multiple missions [from tech development to help desk support],” says Kamaran Sheikh, director of the CIO: Partner Ecosystem at IBM.
