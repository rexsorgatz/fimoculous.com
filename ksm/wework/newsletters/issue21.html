ISSUE 21 • May 26, 2020
How can we keep people healthy in commercial buildings?
As cities around the world start to get a handle on the coronavirus pandemic, companies are slowly returning to the workplace. Working from home has proved effective for many, but even the most productive video call can’t replace the collaboration and innovation that comes with in-person interaction. That said, changes are needed—and landlords and occupiers are taking measures to keep employees healthy in commercial buildings. WeWork is also enhancing our spaces to ensure everyone feels comfortable and safe coming to work.
MEMBERS SPOTLIGHT
Ceca Foundation, RelayCare, meinRezept
Washington, D.C., Hamburg
Even amid the uncertainty of a global pandemic, three WeWork members are working toward a more hopeful future in healthcare. Washington, D.C.–based Ceca Foundation (an abbreviation of “celebrating caregivers”) has launched a special award series to reward unsung COVID-19 caregivers. Also in D.C., RelayCare has added information on coronavirus testing sites to its healthcare app. Meanwhile, in Germany, Hamburg startup meinRezept is streamlining the process of coronavirus testing by offering a telehealth experience that takes users from symptom confirmation through to receiving treatment prescriptions in the mail. Read their stories on Ideas by WeWork.
