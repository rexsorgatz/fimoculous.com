How do we maintain fresh air indoors for a safe return to work?
HVAC (which stands for heating, ventilation, and air-conditioning) systems control the amount of air in an indoor area, the cleanliness of that air, and the overall thermal comfort that the air can provide. As cities cautiously allow employees to return to the office, these systems will play a part in ensuring employees’ health and safety indoors. In every location, WeWork is ramping up efforts to maximize fresh, filtered air. WeWork’s global head of MEP (mechanical, electrical, and plumbing work) explains how and what that means for your business.

Peek Inside
Blurring the lines between outdoors and in
The impact of nature is indisputable—plants and flowers bring emotional joy, fresh air, and an overall sense of well-being. Biophilic design takes the idea of a room full of succulents a few steps further, relying on strong guidance from nature in all aspects of building design. Lights brighten and dim throughout the day. Corridors look like forests or valleys, using those ecosystems’ materials and colors. Sounds echo those heard in a park or on a trail. Use the elements of this design movement to inspire and enhance your own space—whether at home or at the office.
