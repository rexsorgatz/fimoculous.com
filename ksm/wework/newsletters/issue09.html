This ‘Shark Tank’ winner proves that profit and purpose are not mutually exclusive
John Sorial founded TaDah Foods—a WeWork member at WeWork 1775 Tysons Blvd that makes delicious, plant-based frozen foods inspired by Lebanese and Egyptian cuisine—to be more than just a food company. More than 25 percent of profits is donated to charities. “It’s always been about the food, but the deeper meaning is that food is this part of the social fabric that really connects us,” he says. This fact surprised many of the Sharks when Sorial appeared on the season 11 premiere of ABC’s Shark Tank. Still, Sorial found a partner who also saw food as an agent for social change, striking a deal with special guest Daniel Lubetzky, founder of Kind Snacks.

How Sprint improved employee collaboration by upgrading their workspace
Sprint was looking to transform its 156,000-square-foot campus headquarters in Overland Park, Kansas, into a comfortable and welcoming home, but with the energy and amenities of a vibrant, modern workspace for over 1,000 employees. “WeWork forced us to think about space very differently—and not just physical space, but the experiences you can create in those spaces,” says Deeanne King, chief human resources officer at Sprint. The new space has open floor plans for collaboration as well as quiet nooks for solo work. The décor reflects local culture and celebrates the unique spirit of Kansas City. King notes that the WeWork community team has helped welcome employees into the space and ensure that they’re happy, productive, and thriving.
