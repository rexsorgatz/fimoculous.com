ISSUE 11 • Jan 6, 2019
How to grow your business in 2020
It’s no surprise that WeWork members have set some ambitious business goals in the New Year: scaling and expanding into new business areas; increasing revenue; prioritizing sustainability and employee well-being; and facilitating connections, to name a few. The objectives may be tailored to each member company, including an influencer marketing agency, an educational media company, a plant-based food company, and others. Yet we can all learn from the strategies they’re executing to achieve them: doing fewer, bigger deals; pursuing strategic growth partnerships; cultivating community among customers; spending quality time with clients; and more.
MEMBER SPOTLIGHT
LJ Hooker
Sydney, Australia
LJ Hooker, one of the largest real estate groups in Australia, was looking for a new office that would better allow its employees to work together. When the team saw WeWork 161 Castlereagh St, they knew it was the right place for them. The office is located in Sydney’s Central Business District, features sweeping views of the harbor, and includes thoughtful design touches to foster collaboration. The space has ushered in a positive cultural shift at the company. “People are just naturally having more corridor conversations,” says Ruth Trewhella, chief operating officer at LJ Hooker. “It really allows everyone to come together and problem-solve on the fly. And then everyone is more productive in the long run.”

Here’s why your next meeting should be held in the office kitchen
There’s a reason why, when you have a party, everyone tends to gather in the kitchen. An office kitchen is no different, serving as an informal meeting place for brainstorming, quick in-person interactions, and team huddles when the conference room is booked––as well as a spot for lunch, of course.
